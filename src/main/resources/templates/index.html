Welcome to CryptoSociety

Cryptography. Half-art, half-science, the practice of hidden writing has existed for thousands of years. It is referenced in holy books, philosophical works, military treatises, and royal correspondence. It has been practiced by kings, priests, scientists, occultists, soldiers, rebels, and spies. Today, we all rely on cryptography to safeguard our bank accounts, online identities, and personal details from criminals and surveillance programs. But what exactly is this obscure means of communication, so ubiquitous in our lives, and yet so beyond understanding that it can seem akin to magic?

This site aims to give an introduction to simple cryptography, regardless of your learning style. If you are completely new to anything to do with hidden writing, please take a look at our walkthrough page, for a guide to some terms and an explanation of what you might find on the site. If you prefer your learning to be more hands-on, take a look at the challenges page, to try your hand at cracking a few ciphers by yourself. And if you're coming here from another site or project, and have some ciphers from somewhere else to crack, please make use of our cipher tool to assist you.

At this point, we should take the time to clarify: the cipher systems discussed on this page come from antiquity. They represent a branch of cryptography known as "classical cryptography." They have all been broken many, many times. Absolutely none of these ciphers will keep your information safe in the modern world. We do not get into modern digital cryptography here. We will not be covering RSA, PGP, hash sums, or anything of that nature. This site is for education, history, and entertainment. Do not use anything you learn here to encrypt your password, bank information, etc.
